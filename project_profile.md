# Project Profile: KREXON

## One-liner
KREXON is a two-layer loyalty exchange — HACD Stack for credible token formation, Solana for real-time AMM swaps — turning $700B in trapped loyalty points into a liquid, interoperable economy.

---

## Category
**Utility / RWA (Real World Asset)**

Loyalty points are a real-world liability on corporate balance sheets. KREXON tokenizes that liability into a liquid, tradeable asset with a credible, PoW-backed origin.

---

## Problem

The global loyalty points market exceeds $700B in outstanding value. Yet:

- **Points expire** without redemption, destroying value for holders
- **Redemption is siloed** — airline miles cannot be swapped for hotel credits without going through a program's own portal at unfavorable rates
- **No secondary market exists** — points holders cannot exit a program they no longer use
- **Trust is centralized** — the program issuer can change point values, restrict redemptions, or shut down entirely

Existing blockchain attempts have failed because they replaced one form of centralized trust (program issuer) with another (token deployer). A Solana SPL token deployed in seconds carries no more credibility than a database entry.

---

## Asset concept

**KREXON (KRX)** — a HACD Stack Asset used as the base liquidity and governance token of the KREXON DEX.

- Formed through HACD Stack issuance on Hacash
- Each KREXON unit traces to a specific HACD lot with visible formation cost
- Bridged or wrapped to Solana for AMM participation
- Used for: governance voting, liquidity incentive rewards, fee capture, and partner access tiers

---

## Why HACD

Standard token deployments answer the question *"can you deploy a token?"* with yes.

HACD Stack answers the question *"can you prove this token was formed with real economic cost, provable scarcity, and on-chain history?"* with yes.

That second question is the one loyalty program partners — hotels, airlines, retailers — will actually ask. HACD is the only issuance layer that answers it credibly.

---

## Target users

**Primary (B2B pitch):**
- Hotel loyalty programs (e.g. major hospitality chains)
- Airline frequent flyer programs
- Retail reward programs (credit card points, store credits)

**Secondary (end user):**
- Points holders who want to exit, swap, or earn yield on dormant loyalty balances
- Crypto-native liquidity providers who want exposure to real-world loyalty asset flows
